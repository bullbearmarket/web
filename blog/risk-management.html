ENGLISH VERSION

What is Risk Management in Trading?

Risk management is the most important skill in trading. It is the system that protects your capital from large losses. Most beginners focus only on profit, but professional traders focus on risk first.

Without proper risk management, even the best strategy will fail in the long run.

PART 1 – FUNDAMENTALS OF RISK MANAGEMENT

1. Why Risk Management is Important?

Imagine you have ₹10,000 capital. If you lose 50%, your capital becomes ₹5,000. Now to recover, you need 100% return, which is very difficult.

That’s why protecting capital is more important than making profit.

2. The 1% Rule

Professional traders risk only 1% or 2% of their capital per trade.

Example: Capital = ₹10,000 Risk per trade = 1% = ₹100

Even if you lose 10 trades, you still have most of your capital safe.

3. Stop Loss – Your Lifeline

Stop loss is a predefined level where you exit the trade to avoid bigger losses.

Real Example: You buy a stock at ₹100 You set stop loss at ₹95 Your maximum loss = ₹5 per share

Without stop loss, one bad trade can destroy your account.

4. Risk-Reward Ratio

This is the ratio between how much you risk and how much you gain.

Example: Risk = ₹100 Target = ₹200 Risk:Reward = 1:2

Even if you win only 50% trades, you still stay profitable.

5. Position Sizing

Never invest all money in one trade.

Example: Capital = ₹10,000 Risk = ₹100 Stop loss distance = ₹5 Position size = 20 shares

This keeps your risk controlled.

PART 2 – ADVANCED RISK MANAGEMENT

6. Capital Allocation

Divide your capital into multiple trades. Never put all money in one stock.

Example: ₹10,000 capital Trade 1 → ₹3,000 Trade 2 → ₹3,000 Trade 3 → ₹4,000

7. Avoid Overtrading

More trades ≠ more profit More trades = more risk

Professional traders take only high-quality trades.

8. Emotional Control

Biggest enemy = emotions

  • Fear → exit early
  • Greed → hold too long
  • Revenge → overtrading

Discipline is key.

9. Real Life Scenario

Trader A risks 20% per trade Trader B risks 1% per trade

After 5 losing trades: Trader A loses most capital Trader B survives easily

Conclusion → survival is success in trading.

10. Golden Rules

  • Always use stop loss
  • Never risk more than 2%
  • Follow risk-reward ratio
  • Protect capital first

Risk management is not optional. It is mandatory.

भाग 1 – बेसिक रिस्क मैनेजमेंट

1. रिस्क मैनेजमेंट क्यों जरूरी है?

अगर आपके पास ₹10,000 हैं और आप 50% loss कर देते हैं, तो आपके पास ₹5,000 बचते हैं।

अब recovery के लिए आपको 100% profit चाहिए, जो बहुत मुश्किल है।

2. 1% नियम

हर ट्रेड में सिर्फ 1% risk लो।

Example: Capital = ₹10,000 Risk = ₹100

3. स्टॉप लॉस

स्टॉप लॉस आपकी सुरक्षा है।

Example: आपने ₹100 पर शेयर खरीदा Stop loss = ₹95 Loss = ₹5

4. रिस्क-रिवार्ड

हमेशा 1:2 या उससे ज्यादा रखना चाहिए।

Risk ₹100 → Profit ₹200

5. पोजिशन साइजिंग

सारा पैसा एक ट्रेड में मत लगाओ।
